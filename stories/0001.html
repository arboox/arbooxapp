If you've ever been to Chicago —or seen a satellite picture, you might have noticed some buildings in the city have implemented the so-called "rooftop gardens". These gardens are a solution to a set of problems that are harming the planet. In this story, we share the secret sauce behind rooftop gardens and the troubles they're solving.



What is a rooftop garden?


A rooftop garden is, as its name indicates, a garden sitting on top of a roof. You can see these gardens in hotels and corporate buildings, but some citizens have implemented them in duplexes and houses.




Building complex with rooftop gardens — Unsplash



What are the benefits of hosting a rooftop garden?


There are many benefits that creating a rooftop garden can bring: community engagement, business exposure and clean air, to name some, but one of the most important benefits is the versatility that rooftop gardens offer.




This is Tony's Rooftop Bar at Time Out Market in Chicago — Photo by Kevin Serna




Tony's Rooftop Bar is just a great example of how people can transform rooftop gardens to become better spaces. Restaurants are not the only way to transform a rooftop garden, but also orchards, small parks, summer camp spaces, and many more!



Which problems are rooftop gardens helping to solve?


Rooftop gardens play an important role in decreasing overall noise in busy urban areas. Empty roofs in buildings absorb and then radiate heat. On the other hand, rooftop gardens help to cool buildings, reducing carbon emissions.
